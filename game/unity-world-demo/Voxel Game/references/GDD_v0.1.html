Game Design Document — v0.1

The Voxel Kingdom

A seamless fantasy world where you are the hero, the general, and the lord

Engine: Godot 4 Genre: Action RPG / Strategy Setting: Fantasy Medieval Mode: Single Player
01

Vision & North Star

Core Design Statement

"A seamless blend between first-person action exploration — the feel of Zelda: Breath of the Wild — and the nation-scale command of Total War: Warhammer, built inside a living voxel world. At any moment the player can be picking a lock in a dungeon, commanding troops on a hillside, or deciding the fate of a kingdom — without a loading screen between those moments."

The defining challenge and opportunity of this game is the scale transition. Most games lock you into one scale: you are either a hero OR a general OR a ruler. This game makes you all three simultaneously, and the transitions between scales should feel natural and earned, not jarring.

The closest existing game is Mount & Blade: Bannerlord — but this goes further by putting you fully in first-person during personal combat (à la Dark Souls / Zelda BotW), making the voxel world fully interactive and destructible, and adding a Zelda-style dungeon layer beneath the overworld.

Core Gameplay Pillars

I — The Hero

First-person exploration, direct physical combat, dungeon crawling, and personal quests. The human-scale layer.

II — The General

Commanding squads in field battles. You can be on the front line or step back to direct. The skirmish layer.

III — The Lord

Overworld map, settlements, factions, diplomacy, resource empire. The kingdom layer.

IV — The Builder

Voxel construction — fortifications, homes, infrastructure. The world is yours to reshape.

These four pillars should never feel like separate "modes." The player flows between them organically. Walking your walls as The Lord → spotting an enemy scout → switching to The Hero to engage → calling your troops as The General — all in one continuous session.

03

World Structure

Overworld Map

  • A top-down strategic view of the entire realm
  • Shows kingdoms, settlements, roads, armies
  • Time can pass faster here (like Bannerlord travel)
  • Click a location to zoom in to the voxel world
  • Faction territories shift as the world evolves

Zoomed-In World

  • Full 3D voxel environment — the "ground level"
  • Where all first-person gameplay happens
  • Each region has unique biome feel and architecture
  • Contains surface layer AND underground dungeon layer
  • Voxel terrain is persistent — what you build stays built

World Generation

  • Hand-crafted skeleton — major landmarks, story locations, kingdom capitals are authored
  • Proc-gen fills biomes, terrain variation, dungeon layouts, resource placement
  • No two playthroughs have the same geography outside the fixed points
  • World seed system — shareable seeds for consistent worlds

Biome Types (planned)

  • Temperate highlands — the main starting region
  • Dense forest — ancient, dark, enemy-heavy
  • Badlands / scarred earth — corrupted or war-torn
  • Coastal cliffs — sea trade routes
  • Mountain range — natural borders, dwarven ruins

04

Combat Systems

Design Target

Two distinct but connected combat layers. Personal combat is kinetic and skill-based. Army combat is tactical and spatial. The player chooses how deeply to engage with each at any moment.

Personal Combat — "The Hero Layer"

  • First-person, direct and physical (Mount & Blade feel)
  • Directional blocking — stance matters
  • Weapon weight affects swing speed and stamina
  • Mounted combat supported (horse changes everything)
  • Magic integrated as a combat tool (spells, not a separate mode)
  • Death has consequence — not a simple respawn

Army Combat — "The General Layer"

  • Squads of 10–30 units per side
  • You can fight alongside your troops in first-person
  • Or step back and issue commands from a pulled-out view
  • Unit types: infantry, archers, cavalry, mage support
  • Terrain matters — voxel hills, walls, chokepoints
  • Morale system — routing units, fear effects

The key design challenge: the transition between personal and command view during battle must feel seamless. The proposed solution is a hold-key "commander view" that pulls the camera up and slows perceived time slightly — similar to Total War's battle pause — while still showing the real-time action below.

05

Player Progression

Character Skills (RPG Layer)

  • Skill trees across: Combat, Magic, Leadership, Crafting, Stealth
  • Skills improve through use — swing a sword more, get better at it
  • Milestone skill points for major choices (like Total War hero skills)
  • No class lock-in — hybrid builds are valid
  • Leadership skills directly affect army performance

Kingdom Progression (Strategy Layer)

  • Settlement tiers: camp → village → town → city → fortress
  • Each tier unlocks new unit types, buildings, and trade routes
  • Influence/reputation system per faction
  • Vassal system — other lords can swear to you
  • Tech/knowledge tree for crafting and magic research

06

Factions & World Politics

The world has multiple competing factions/kingdoms operating on the overworld map. They are autonomous — they expand, war, form alliances, and fall without the player's involvement. The player enters a living world already in motion.

Planned Faction Archetypes

Human kingdom (the default "civilized" power), a militaristic empire, a tribal/raider culture, an isolationist magical order, and a neutral merchant league. Names and lore TBD.

Faction Relations

War, peace, non-aggression, and alliance states. Player actions shift faction opinions. Reputation is persistent and earned, not purchased.

Player's Faction

Player can be independent, join an existing faction, or found their own. All three paths are viable across a full playthrough.

07

Dungeons & Underground Layer

Every region of the surface world has a corresponding underground layer. Dungeons are the Zelda layer of this game — self-contained adventure spaces with puzzles, secrets, enemies, and rewards that feel distinct from the overworld.

Dungeon Structure

  • Proc-gen layouts with hand-authored set pieces and bosses
  • Multi-floor — surface cave → deep ruins → ancient vaults
  • Voxel walls can be mined — secrets hidden in geometry
  • Environmental puzzles using voxel physics
  • Boss encounters tied to region lore

Dungeon Rewards

  • Rare crafting materials
  • Unique weapons and armor
  • Magical tomes / spell unlocks
  • Lore fragments that build world narrative
  • Ancient structures player can claim and build around

08

Magic System

Magic exists in the world and the player can learn and use it, but it has weight and cost. It should never trivialize combat — it should change the way you fight, not eliminate challenge.

Magic Principles

  • Learned — spells are found, studied, or taught
  • Costly — stamina, health, or resource drain
  • Contextual — some spells only work in certain conditions
  • Visible in the world — magical ruins, corrupted zones, ley lines

Magic Schools (planned)

  • Elemental — fire, ice, lightning (combat-focused)
  • Ward — shields, buffs, support
  • Arcane — utility, unlocking, sensing hidden things
  • Necromantic — forbidden, powerful, reputation consequences

09

Building & Settlement System

Voxel Construction

  • Free-form voxel building anywhere in the world
  • Block types tied to resource gathering
  • Structural integrity — overhangs and spans have limits
  • Defenses: walls, towers, gates, murder holes
  • Enemy armies can besiege and damage your structures

Settlement Management

  • NPCs inhabit and work in your settlements
  • Assign roles: farmer, soldier, blacksmith, trader
  • Supply chains — food, weapons, materials flow between settlements
  • Happiness / unrest system — neglected towns rebel
  • Trade routes to other factions generate passive income

10

Narrative & Story

The game is sandbox-first — the world runs and is interesting without the player triggering any story content. But an optional main quest exists for players who want a directed narrative experience.

Sandbox World Events

  • Factions rise and fall dynamically
  • Random events: famines, plagues, legendary monsters appearing
  • NPC lords have ambitions that play out over time
  • The world has history that accumulates — a chronicle of what happened

Optional Main Quest

  • An ancient threat stirring beneath the dungeon layer
  • Uncovered gradually through dungeon exploration and lore fragments
  • Can be ignored indefinitely — but the threat grows over time
  • Resolving it doesn't end the game — the world continues after

12

Implementation Phases — Ground Up

1

World Foundation

The voxel engine, terrain, atmosphere, and player movement. Proves the world feels good to be in.

  • Chunk-based voxel terrain system
  • Hand-crafted demo region (rolling hills, forest, cliffs)
  • First-person player controller with collision
  • Day/night cycle, fog, ambient lighting
  • One dungeon entrance with a short corridor
  • HTML5 + desktop export working
2

Personal Combat

The hero layer. Player can fight enemies in first-person. Dungeon has actual enemies and rewards.

  • Directional melee combat system
  • Basic enemy AI (patrol, detect, attack)
  • Health, stamina, death handling
  • First dungeon floor with combat and a boss
  • Basic inventory and loot drops
3

Settlement & Building

The lord layer. Player can place and manage a basic settlement. NPCs inhabit it.

  • Voxel building placement system
  • NPC spawning and basic role assignment
  • Resource gathering and storage
  • Settlement tier 1 — camp to village
4

Army & Skirmish Combat

The general layer. Player can recruit, command, and fight alongside squads.

  • Unit recruitment from settlement
  • Basic squad command (follow, hold, advance)
  • First skirmish scenario — defend the camp
  • Commander view toggle
  • Morale and routing behavior
5

Overworld Map & Factions

The kingdom layer. Multiple factions, travel, and diplomacy on a strategic map.

  • Overworld map UI
  • 2–3 rival faction kingdoms with basic AI behavior
  • Travel between regions
  • Reputation and diplomacy system
  • World events — raids, trade caravans, faction wars
6

RPG Progression & Magic

Character depth. Skills, magic, and long-term player growth.

  • Skill system across combat, leadership, magic, crafting
  • Magic schools — elemental and ward to start
  • Main quest hook — first lore fragments surface
  • Multiple dungeon types across different regions
  • Full crafting system
